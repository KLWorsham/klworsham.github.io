Public Knowledge

Kit is a Technology & Policy Fellow at Public Knowledge where he works on net neutrality and data use, patent and copyright, and other telecom concerns. He has been involved with researching and developing an evidentiary record for the Federal Communications Commission hearing on net neutrality and Title II of the Communications Act.

He develops educational materials for audiences with varying levels of technical proficiency. Such materials include diagrams and graphics that explain complex technical issues in a manner that is easily understood by individuals without a sophisticated technical background. His projects have included patent reform, music licensing, and internet freedom.

Copyright is anothis area of focus. He has researched and developed arguments regarding software copyright and web scrapers. In particular, he has emphasized how legal precedent relying on outdated technologies are not applicable to modern cases of internet use and copyright.

He is also involved with educating Congressional staff and evaluating their policy positions with regards to telecom issues. His work includes ascertaining audience bias and tailoring communication to best effectuate persuasion.

Army JAG Corps

Kit has also worked with the Army JAG Corps where he worked with the UCMJ and general applications of military law. His assignments included Fort Belvoir Defense Appellate Division and Fort Meyer-Mcnair Military Justice, Special Victims Unit, and Legal Assistance Office.

At the Fort Belvoir Defense Appellate Division, Kit researched Constitutional issues such as privacy rights, search and seizure limitations, and the right against self incrimination. In particular, he handled questions involving smart phone login capabilities, where the police had compelled a servicemember to enter his password.

At the Special Victims Unit, he researched subpoena requirements for compelling trial testimony. Because victim testimony is often essential to sexual assault cases, the goal was to to successfully elicit of trial testimony from sexual assault victims while still maintaining sensitivity and respect for victims' wishes.

His work at Military Justice included developing prosecution strategies, organizing evidence, and drafting opening statements.

Gulf Recovery Network

Kit volunteered with the Gulf Recovery Netowrk George Washington University Law. During his pro bono assignment, worked with the Gulf Coast Center for Law & Policy (GCCLP) in Slidell, Louisiana. He primarily assisted indigent clients with immigration issues such as green card reinstatement and asylum petitions.

Emory Office of LGBT Life

Kit worked at the Emory Office of LGBT Life promoting the office's inclusionary goals. He designed promotional materials for office events and assisted with panels that sought to educate the college community on diversity concerns. He also established collaborative projects with other university organizations to host and promote events.

Carlos Museum Education Department

The Education Department at the Carlos Museum offers a wide variety of public educational programs. Kit worked as both a designer and audio visual technician for speaker events. He created flyers and other promotional graphics for museum events and also prepared materials for children's workshops. This included creating templates for clay molds and ink etchings.

In addition to handling lighting and audio-video equipment, he also managed file transfers and content display compatability for guest speaker presentations.
